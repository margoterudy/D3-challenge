Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

This graph shows the association between different health factors of smokers and their age according to each US state. The data was collected from the American Community Survey (ACS) and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) during the year of 2014. Each circle represents US state.

Per the data given, it can be found that Utah has the least amount of smokers. The state with the most amount of smokers is West Virginia.

Maine maintains the oldest average smoker at around 44 years of age, and UT holds the youngest average smoker at around 30 years old.

In general, the average smoking age in the United States during 2014 was about 38 years old (or between 36 and 40).
